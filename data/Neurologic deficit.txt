 A neurologic deficit refers to abnormal neurologic function of a body area. This altered function is due to injury of the brain, spinal cord, muscles, or nerves that feed the affected area.Examples include:Abnormal reflexesInability to speakDecreased sensationLoss of balanceMental function problems, such as memory loss or difficulty with thought processingVision changesWalking problemsWeakness of the face, arms or legsImages

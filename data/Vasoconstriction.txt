 Vasoconstriction is the narrowing (constriction) of blood vessels by small muscles in their walls. When blood vessels constrict, blood flow is slowed or blocked.Vasoconstriction may be slight or severe. It may result from disease, medicines, or psychological conditions. Medicines that cause vasoconstriction include:Decongestants, including pseudoephedrineCough and cold combinationsMedicines may be used to increase or reduce vasoconstriction in very ill people.Images

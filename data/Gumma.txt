 A gumma is a soft, tumor-like growth of the tissues (granuloma) that occurs in people with syphilis.Causes

A gumma is caused by the bacteria that cause syphilis. It appears during late-stage tertiary syphilis. It most often contains a mass of dead and swollen fiber-like tissue. It is most often seen in the liver. It also can occur in the:BoneBrainHeartSkinTestisEyes Similar-looking sores sometimes occur with tuberculosis.Images

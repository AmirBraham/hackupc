 Today, many parents wonder if it is wise for children to have their tonsils taken out. Tonsillectomy may be recommended if your child has any of the following:Difficulty swallowingObstructed breathing during sleepThroat infections or throat abscesses that keep returning In most cases, inflammation of the tonsils can be successfully treated with antibiotics. There are always risks associated with surgery.You and your child's health care provider may consider a tonsillectomy if:Your child has frequent infections (7 or more times in 1 year, 5 or more times per year over 2 years, or 3 or more times per year over 3 years).Your child misses a lot of school.Your child snores, has trouble breathing, and has sleep apnea.Your child has an abscess or growth on their tonsils.

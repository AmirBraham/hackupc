 The hypothalamus is an area of the brain that produces hormones that control:Body temperatureHeart rateHungerMoodRelease of hormones from many glands, especially the pituitary glandSex driveSleepThirstInformation

HYPOTHALAMIC DISEASEHypothalamic dysfunction can occur as a result of diseases, including:Genetic causes (often present at birth or during childhood)Infection or inflammationInjury as a result of trauma, surgery or radiationSYMPTOMS OF HYPOTHALAMIC DISEASEBecause the hypothalamus controls so many different functions, hypothalamic disease can have many different symptoms, depending on the cause. The most common symptoms are:Increased appetite and rapid weight gainExtreme thirst and frequent urination (diabetes insipidus)Low body temperatureSlow heart rateImages

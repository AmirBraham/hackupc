 Lymphoid hyperplasia is an increase in the number of normal cells contained in the lymph nodes. These cells are called lymphocytes. This can occur as a reaction to infection with bacteria, viruses, or other types of germs. This may or may not be normal, depending on why it occurs.Alternative Names
